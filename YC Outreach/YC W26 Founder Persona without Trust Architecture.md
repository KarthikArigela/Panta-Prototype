# ICP Persona: YC W26 Startup Founder

## The Composite Profile

**Name:** "The Galvanized Founder"
**Archetype:** Battle-tested builder under extreme time pressure
**Demo Day:** March 24, 2026
**Current Phase:** Post-acceptance, pre-demo day sprint

---

## Identity Snapshot

A founder who has likely:
- Been rejected before (YC or elsewhere)
- Pivoted multiple times before finding this idea
- Moved cities, burned boats, or made irreversible bets
- Built something with minimal resources before getting funded
- Values trust built over time over impressive credentials

**Core belief:** "Obsession moves people, not polish."

**Self-image:** A ball of dough being pressed through an extruder—actively being transformed by pressure into something harder and more capable.

---

## Core Job To Be Done

> "Help me ship faster than I thought possible so I have undeniable traction by demo day."

**Underlying JTBD:** Prove this company deserves to exist before the world decides it doesn't.

---

## Deep Pains (High Confidence)

### Surface Pains (What They'll Say)
- "We need to ship more features faster"
- "I'm drowning in execution, not enough hands"
- "Every day we don't launch is a day wasted"

### Hidden Pains (What They Feel)
- **Fear of mediocrity:** Demo day is a forcing function—they know hundreds of startups will present, most will be forgotten
- **The clock is always ticking:** They feel time differently. A week feels like a month. Waiting feels like dying.
- **Co-founder fragility:** They know co-founder breakups kill more startups than competition. Every hire affects this dynamic.
- **Imposter syndrome masked as confidence:** They got in with less than they expected. Now they have to prove they belong.

---

## What They Actually Value (When Evaluating People)

| Signal | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Work shown before asked** | Mirrors how they got into YC—obsessive preparation, not polished pitches |
| **Domain understanding** | They spent weeks learning their industry before building. They respect the same. |
| **Speed of execution** | "Traditional process: 3 weeks. Our AI agent: minutes." They think in compression ratios. |
| **Low ego, high output** | They've been rejected. They don't want someone who hasn't been humbled. |
| **Trust signals > credentials** | "You can't build a company on credentials. You need trust." — Literal quote from Vincent |

---

## Triggers & Decision Moments

**When they actually read cold outreach:**

1. **When it shows work, not pitch** — A live prototype beats a resume every time
2. **When it demonstrates domain obsession** — "I taught myself X in 5 days" mirrors their own origin story
3. **When the ask is asymmetric** — "Work free for 2 weeks, yours to keep either way" removes risk
4. **When timing aligns with their bottleneck** — They're thinking about what blocks them THIS week

**When they ignore cold outreach:**

- Generic compliments ("Your vision is inspiring")
- Resume-first approaches
- Requests for their time without offering value first
- Any hint of "I'd love to learn more about the role"

---

## Decision-Making Psychology

### How They Evaluate (Fast)
They're scanning for pattern-match with themselves:
- "Did this person do what I would do?"
- "Did they show obsession before asking for permission?"
- "Can I verify their work without a call?"

### The 30-Second Test
Your outreach gets 30 seconds. In that window:
1. Is there a clickable prototype? (Yes/No)
2. Does it relate to MY problem? (Yes/No)
3. Did they understand something non-obvious about my space? (Yes/No)

If all three: they read the rest. If not: delete.

## What Convinces Them

### Proof Types That Work

| Proof Type | Example | Why It Works |
|------------|---------|--------------|
| **Working prototype** | Live demo link, GitHub repo | "Show, don't tell" — their operating principle |
| **Domain depth** | Specific insight about their industry's bottleneck | Mirrors their own YC interview prep |
| **Compression ratio** | "26-day process → 20 minutes" | They think in 10x, not 10% |
| **Self-awareness** | "I didn't know what ACORD 125 was 6 days ago" | Honesty about learning curve signals growth mindset |

### Proof Types That Don't Work

- Years of experience (unless directly relevant)
- List of technologies known
- Prestigious previous employers
- Generic problem-solving claims

---

## Communication Preferences

### Language They Use
- "Ship it"
- "Surface area for luck"
- "Maximizing ___"
- "We're just getting started"
- "The bottleneck is ___"
- "Ball of dough → pasta" (transformation through pressure)

### Language That Turns Them Off
- "I'd love to pick your brain"
- "Let me know if you're hiring"
- "I'm passionate about startups"
- "Quick question" (nothing is quick right now)

### Format Preferences
- **Short > long** (they're time-starved)
- **Async-verifiable** (they can check your work without scheduling a call)
- **Mobile-readable** (they're not at desks)

---

## The Trust Ladder

How trust builds with this persona:

```
Level 0: Stranger with a pitch (ignored)
    ↓
Level 1: Stranger who did work first (gets 30 seconds)
    ↓
Level 2: Someone who understood something non-obvious (gets a reply)
    ↓
Level 3: Someone who executes well with minimal guidance (gets a trial)
    ↓
Level 4: Someone they can trust with autonomy (gets the role)
```

Your prototype + cold outreach is trying to skip from Level 0 → Level 2.

## Anti-Persona Traits (Disqualifiers)

These signals make them immediately disengage:

- **Credential-leading:** Opens with school/company names
- **Time-asking before value-giving:** "Can we hop on a call?"
- **Generic spray-and-pray:** Same message to 50 startups
- **Risk-shifting:** "I'd need to understand the role better before..."
- **Perfectionism signals:** Over-polished but shallow

Beneath the hustle:
- **Exhausted but wired** — Running on momentum, coffee, and cold plunges
- **Quietly terrified** — Demo day is coming and there's never enough time
- **Defensive of their time** — Every minute spent on hiring is a minute not shipping
- **Hungry for believers** — They want people who get it without extensive explanation
